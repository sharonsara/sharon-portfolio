A new AI tool ships roughly every hour now. I can't try them all properly, so I run a
fast triage to decide whether something earns real hours. Here's the exact checklist.

## The 30-minute pass

1. **Give it my hardest real task first.** Not the demo task — the messy one from my
   actual week. Demos are designed to win; real work tells the truth.
2. **Watch where it hesitates.** The first place it gets vague is where the product is thin.
3. **Break it on purpose.** Ambiguous input, missing context, a contradiction. How it
   fails matters more than how it succeeds.
4. **Check the second answer.** Anyone can get lucky once. Consistency is the signal.
5. **Ask: would I reach for this again Monday?** If I hesitate, the answer is no.

## What I ignore

Pricing pages, hype threads, and the polished launch video. None of them predict whether
a tool survives contact with a real task.

Thirty minutes, one honest task. That's usually all it takes to know.
