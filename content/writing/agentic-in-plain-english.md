"Agentic" is the word of the year and almost nobody defines it before using it.
Here's the whole idea without the hype.

## The plain version

A normal AI tool answers one question at a time. You ask, it replies, done.
An **agentic** system is given a *goal* instead of a single question — and it's allowed to
take several steps on its own to reach it: look something up, try an action, check the result,
adjust, try again.

The difference isn't intelligence. It's **autonomy over multiple steps.**

## A kitchen analogy

- A regular model is a chef who answers "how do I make pasta?"
- An agentic system is a chef you tell "make me dinner," who then checks the fridge,
  decides on a dish, cooks it, tastes it, and re-salts — without asking you between each step.

## Why it matters to you

More autonomy means more usefulness *and* more room for it to go off-course. The skill
shifts from "ask a good question" to "set a good goal and a good boundary." That's the
whole game from here.
