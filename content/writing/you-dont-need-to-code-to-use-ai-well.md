People keep asking me which programming language to learn before they "get into AI."
Wrong question. The skill that actually moves the needle is being able to say,
in plain words, exactly what you want — and then noticing where the model misread you.

That's it. That's the job.

## Why this is good news

The barrier was never the syntax. It was the thinking. If you can write a clear brief
for a freelancer, you can already direct an AI tool. The people getting the most out of
these systems right now aren't the best engineers — they're the clearest communicators.

## Where to start instead

- Write the request the way you'd explain it to a sharp friend who has no context.
- When the output is wrong, don't retype — figure out *which sentence* the model misunderstood.
- Keep a note of the phrasings that consistently work. That's your real toolkit.

Coding helps once you want to wire tools together. But to *use* AI well? You already have what it takes.
